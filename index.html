About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue University, advised by Prof. Vijay Raghunathan. My research sits at the intersection of machine learning and computer architecture, with a focus on making AI systems dramatically more energy-efficient for deployment at the edge.

I have worked on enabling complex AI workloads — including graph neural networks, state space models, and multimodal cognitive systems — to run efficiently on resource-constrained neural processing units (NPUs). My work spans the full stack: from algorithmic approximations to hardware-software co-design to silicon-aware optimization.

Prior to Purdue, I spent time at Intel (Client AI Research), Texas Instruments (Design Verification & Validation), and IIT Kharagpur (Research Intern), building a rare breadth across industry and academia.
